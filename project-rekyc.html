PROBLEM & APPROACH

🔥 The Problem
Education lending has a compliance problem. RBI mandates periodic RE-KYC for all borrowers, but the process was entirely manual — compliance officers reviewing documents one by one, flagging risk based on intuition. High-risk users were slipping through. Low-risk users were being over-scrutinised. And with no audit trail, every regulatory review was a scramble.
💡 The Approach
I led the build of a modular compliance platform: automated document parsing via OCR, real-time verification against DigiLocker + Aadhaar + PAN APIs, and a Dynamic Risk Tier engine using behavioral signals — unusual repayment cycles, location anomalies, transaction velocity spikes — to reclassify users in real-time. Compliance officers got a dashboard. Auditors got a full trail.
Risk Model

DYNAMIC RISK TIERS

Low Risk
Consistent repayment history, stable location, transaction patterns within expected range. Annual RE-KYC sufficient.
✓ On-time repayments 12+ months
✓ Location stable
✓ No anomalous transactions
Medium Risk
Minor anomalies detected. Triggered for 6-month review cycle with document refresh required.
⚠ 1–2 late repayments
⚠ New location detected
⚠ Velocity spike (minor)
High Risk
Multiple anomaly signals. Immediate compliance review triggered with full document re-verification and manual officer assignment.
🚨 Multiple missed repayments
🚨 Unusual tx velocity
🚨 Document anomaly detected

PM Thinking

KEY DECISIONS

🤔 Build vs Buy
We evaluated 3 third-party KYC vendors. All of them handled verification well. None handled the dynamic re-scoring based on post-onboarding behavior. That's the defensible part — the ongoing risk intelligence, not the one-time check. We built the scoring layer in-house and integrated vendor APIs for document verification only.
📐 Compliance First, Always
Every feature went through a FATF compliance review before shipping. The rule versioning system — where every scoring decision logs the exact rule version used — was added after our first internal audit showed we couldn't explain a tier change. Explainability isn't a nice-to-have in a regulated product. It's the product.
