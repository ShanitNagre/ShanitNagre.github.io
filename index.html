How I Think
MY APPROACH
TO AI PRODUCTS
01
🔁
Migrate, Don't Just Automate
Every project I've worked on has been a migration — taking a process people had been doing manually for years and rebuilding it on modern ML. The hard part isn't the model. It's the change management. Getting ops teams to trust a system that was wrong 8% of the time when they were wrong 22% of the time — that's the actual product problem.
02
📐
Instrument Before You Launch
I've seen AI features ship with zero logging and die quietly. My rule: if you can't measure it in a dashboard on day one, it's not ready to ship. I set up eval loops before the model goes live — precision, recall, latency, escalation rate — all tied to the business metric the feature is supposed to move.
03
🪜
5% First. Always.
Big-bang AI launches make great press releases and bad products. Adoption follows trust, and trust is built in small doses. The claims pipeline went live on 200 claims a week before it touched the full queue. I roll everything out small, measure against human baseline, and expand when the numbers say to.
