Core Product Philosophy
Problem

THE GAP IN FINTECH

🔥 What's broken
Every budgeting app treats money as a math problem. Track your spending. Set a budget. Get a notification when you overspend. It doesn't work — because spending behavior is emotional, not rational. Young professionals know they shouldn't impulse-buy at 11pm when they're stressed. They do it anyway. No app has addressed the emotional layer.
💡 The opportunity
Aivana sits at the intersection of behavioral finance and AI coaching. Instead of tracking what happened, it predicts what's about to happen — using sentiment signals, spending velocity, and life events — and intervenes before the decision is made. Think of it as a financial therapist, not a financial accountant.
Features

CORE CAPABILITIES

🧠
Emotion-Aware Nudges
Sentiment analysis on user behavior patterns combined with transaction velocity to detect emotional spending triggers. Sends contextual nudges before impulsive decisions — not after.
🔮
Life Scenario Simulator
AI-powered "what if" engine. Users simulate financial decisions — buying a car, taking a sabbatical, moving cities — and see the 5-year impact on their financial health before committing.
🎯
Smart Goal Tracking
Goals linked to open banking APIs (via Plaid). Progress updates dynamically based on actual transactions, not manual inputs. AI recalibrates timelines when spending patterns shift.
Experience

USER JOURNEY

01
Connect & Profile
Link bank accounts via Plaid. Aivana builds a baseline financial personality from 90 days of transaction history.
02
AI Diagnosis
Sentiment + spending pattern analysis identifies your top 3 financial risk behaviors — the ones costing you money without realising.
03
Live Coaching
Real-time nudges tied to your calendar, location, and emotional state. "You tend to overspend on food when you work late. Dinner booked tonight — want a ceiling?"
04
Scenario Planning
Monthly life simulation — run "what if I bought a house in 18 months" against your actual trajectory. Adjust goals, see updated milestones.

PM Thinking

WHY THIS PRODUCT

📐 Design Decisions
The hardest call was not building a budgeting feature. Every user test showed people wanted a budget. But budgets don't change behavior — they create guilt. Aivana deliberately skips the budget screen and goes straight to behavioral coaching. This is a product bet: that emotional intelligence is more effective than financial discipline.
📊 Success Metrics
North star: % of users who improved their savings rate in 90 days. Secondary: nudge acceptance rate (did users act on the AI suggestion?), scenario simulation engagement (are users planning ahead?), and 30-day retention. We deliberately avoid DAU — financial wellness is a weekly, not daily, behavior.
