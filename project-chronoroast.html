WHAT IS CHRONOROAST

🎮 The Game
You're a time traveler who keeps running into your past and future self across different historical eras. Your other selves are not happy to see you — they roast you for procrastinating, making bad decisions, and generally not living up to your potential.

You have to roast back. GPT evaluates your comeback. If it's good enough, you unlock a Wordle puzzle for that era. Solve the Wordle, and you move to the next time period. Fail either, and you're stuck in time.
💡 Why I Built It
I wanted to explore how creative + evaluative AI can be combined in a game loop — and I wanted to do it in the most unexpected platform possible. Google Sheets as a game engine is a constraint that forces creative solutions.

The constraint is the point. If you can build a GPT-powered game in Sheets, you can build anything anywhere. It's a proof of concept for using AI creatively in non-traditional environments.

Mechanics

THE GAME LOOP

01
Enter the Era
The Sheets UI loads the era context — Ancient Rome, Medieval England, etc. Your past/future self appears with era-appropriate language and attitude.
02
Receive the Roast
GPT generates a personalised roast targeting your real weaknesses (procrastination, inconsistency). The roast is era-flavored — your medieval self uses old English insults.
03
Type Your Comeback
You type your response. No hints, no suggestions. It has to be genuinely witty — GPT is judging creativity, relevance to the roast, and era-appropriateness.
04
GPT Evaluates
GPT-3.5 scores your comeback on a 0–10 scale with a brief explanation. Score ≥ 7: you unlock the Wordle. Score < 7: you get roasted again harder.
05
Solve the Wordle
Era-themed 5-letter word. Custom color-coded logic in Apps Script (🟩🟨⬜). 6 guesses. Solve it to progress. Fail: stuck in the era until you crack it.
06
Progress or Stay Stuck
Win both → travel to next era. The tone and difficulty escalate as you progress. The future-self roast is genuinely brutal.

Technical

HOW IT'S BUILT

Layer 01 — UI
Google Sheets Interface
Cells styled with conditional formatting to create the game UI. Era headers, input fields, and Wordle grid all built in Sheets. Custom menu items trigger Apps Script functions.
Layer 02 — Logic
Apps Script Engine
Handles all game state: era tracking, score calculation, Wordle validation logic (color-coded feedback), OpenAI API calls, and era transition triggers. ~400 lines of custom script.
Layer 03 — AI
OpenAI GPT-3.5
Two separate API calls per round: one for roast generation (with era + player history as context), one for comeback evaluation (with a structured scoring rubric in the system prompt).
Reflection

WHAT THIS TAUGHT ME

On constraint-driven creativity and AI evaluation
Building a game in Google Sheets sounds ridiculous. That's the point. The constraint forces you to be creative with what you have instead of waiting for the perfect stack. Most product teams I've worked with have way more resources than they need and still ship slowly. ChronoRoast was built in three weekends with zero infrastructure costs.

The more interesting PM lesson was about AI as an evaluator vs AI as a generator. Getting GPT to generate roasts was easy — LLMs are naturally good at that. Getting it to evaluate comebacks consistently and fairly required careful prompt engineering: explicit scoring rubrics, example outputs at each score level, and clear instructions on what creativity means in this context. Evaluation is harder than generation. That's true in games and it's true in enterprise AI products.
